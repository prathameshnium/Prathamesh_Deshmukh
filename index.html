Prathamesh Keshao Deshmukh

PhD Scholar | Condensed Matter Physics

A PhD researcher at the UGC-DAE Consortium for Scientific Research, specializing in the synthesis and characterization of multiferroic composites. My work bridges experimental physics with computational science, focusing on magnetoelectric coupling and the development of novel materials. By utilizing advanced techniques like neutron diffraction and creating Python-based tools for lab automation, I aim to contribute to the next generation of data storage, sensors, and energy-efficient electronics.

Research Profile

Core Research Theme

Multiferroic Composites

My research is centered on fabricating and understanding materials where magnetic and electric properties are intrinsically linked. The goal is to contribute to next-generation sensors, memory devices, and energy-efficient electronics.

Explore My Research

Key Activities

  • Novel material synthesis (e.g., $BaFe_{10}Sc_2O_{19}:KNbO_3$).
  • Design of custom probes for cryogenic measurements.
  • Structural analysis using Neutron & X-Ray Diffraction.
  • Characterization of magnetic and electric properties.
