Tools & Resources

A curated list of software, databases, and online tools that I find essential for research in physics and scientific programming.

Writing & Publication Tools

Overleaf

The standard for collaborative LaTeX writing. Essential for preparing manuscripts, reports, and theses without software installation.

Zotero

An indispensable open-source tool for managing references and bibliographies. The browser extension makes collecting sources effortless.

Shodhganga

A reservoir of Indian theses and dissertations. An excellent resource for literature review and understanding the scope of research in India.

Programming & Version Control

GitHub

The primary platform for version control and collaboration. Crucial for managing code, sharing projects, and tracking changes.

NumPy & SciPy

The foundational libraries for numerical and scientific computing in Python. Essential for any form of data analysis or simulation.

Matplotlib

The standard for creating high-quality, publication-ready plots and figures directly from Python scripts.

Physics Databases & Visualization

Materials Project

An open-access database of material properties computed with DFT. Invaluable for preliminary research and materials discovery.

Crystallography Open DB

A vast open-access collection of crystal structures, perfect for finding reference structures for XRD and neutron diffraction analysis.

VESTA

The standard software for visualizing crystal structures and volumetric data. I use it to create nearly all crystallographic figures.

Online Tools & Calculators

Desmos

An incredibly intuitive and powerful online graphing calculator. Perfect for quickly visualizing functions and exploring mathematical concepts.

NIST Constants

The authoritative source for fundamental physical constants. A reliable and quick reference for any calculation.
